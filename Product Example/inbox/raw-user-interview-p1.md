# Raw Interview Notes — Participant 1
Date: 2026-04-05 | Interviewer: Ana Souza | Duration: ~35 min | Format: Remote, moderated

---

Talked to Marcus, CS manager at a mid-size B2B SaaS. Uses our chatbot for tier-1 support.

Said the bot is "hit or miss" — sometimes nails it, sometimes completely wrong. When it's wrong the customer immediately asks for a human anyway.

Key quote: "the bot responds to what it *hears*, not what the customer *means*."
Example: customer said "my account is locked out" — bot responded with password reset info. Customer had actually been suspended for non-payment. Totally wrong response, different problem entirely.

"The failure isn't that the bot doesn't know the answer. It knows too many answers. It just doesn't know which one applies."

How often does this happen? — estimates 30–40% of tickets that escalate had a wrong first bot response. His agents spend time figuring out what the bot already tried before they can help.

Brazil-based team, a few reps are Portuguese-first. They've given up on the bot for those customers. "It's like it panics when it sees Portuguese."

Friction: bot doesn't learn from agent corrections. "We're training humans to fix bot mistakes, not the other way around."

What would make it better? "If it could just tell me it's not sure, I'd rather it escalate immediately than give a confident wrong answer."
