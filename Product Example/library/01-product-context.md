**Product:** Acme Support AI — AI-powered customer support chatbot for Acme's B2B SaaS platform.

**What it does:** Handles incoming support conversations autonomously, resolving issues without human intervention. When it cannot resolve, it escalates to a human CS agent.

**Who it serves:** Acme's end customers (primarily English-speaking, with a growing LATAM segment). Internal stakeholders: CS team (primary operational partner), ML engineering (2 engineers), Finance (cost sensitivity).

**Current state (Q2 2026):**
- Autonomous resolution rate: 41% (baseline Q1 2026) → target 60% by end of Q2
- Human escalation rate: 59% (target: ≤40%)
- P90 first-response time: 4.2 minutes (target: <60 seconds for resolved tickets)
- Cost-per-ticket: $2.10 (company target: $0.80)

**Why this matters now:** Support costs grew 34% YoY while headcount grew only 12%. The chatbot failing to resolve conversations is the primary driver. Reaching 60% autonomous resolution avoids hiring 8 additional CS agents.

**Primary failure mode identified:** Intent misclassification — the chatbot misidentifies what the user needs, triggering unnecessary escalations. This affects 78% of the user base and accounts for ~60% of failed resolutions. This is what Q2 is solving.

**What this product is NOT doing in Q2:** Multilingual support expansion (affects 22% of users, deferred to Q3), guest checkout flows, or any ML work outside of intent classification.
