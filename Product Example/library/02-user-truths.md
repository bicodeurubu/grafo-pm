## 🔒 Permanent Directives
<!-- LOCK:START — edited only by PM, never auto-updated by agent -->

User insights from our research are ground truth. When a user behavior contradicts an assumption in a spec or proposal, always surface the conflict — never suppress evidence to make a proposal look cleaner.

Research from CS team voice-of-customer is directionally valid but not statistically representative. Treat it as qualitative signal, not quantitative proof.

When evaluating any new feature or change, the first question is always: "Does this help the user get their issue resolved without escalating to a human?" That is the north-star experience metric.

## ⟳ Compiled Context

**Primary user segment:** English-speaking customers of Acme's B2B SaaS platform, initiating support conversations via chatbot. They are not technical — they expect the chatbot to understand their intent without careful phrasing.

**Key behavioral truth #1 — Users blame themselves when the bot fails:**
When the chatbot misclassifies intent, users rephrase and retry. After 2 failed attempts, most escalate to human. They interpret bot failure as "I asked it wrong" — not as a product defect. This means low direct complaint signal despite high failure rate. Do not use low complaint volume as evidence of user satisfaction.

**Key behavioral truth #2 — CS team is a proxy, not a replacement for user research:**
The CS voice-of-customer synthesis reveals what agents observe, not what users experience. Agents notice escalation patterns; they don't see how users feel during the conversation. Use CS input for hypothesis generation, not for validation.

**Key behavioral truth #3 — LATAM users face compounding failures:**
LATAM customers experience both intent misclassification (same as English users) AND language mismatch. Multilingual gap is deferred to Q3, but when communicating product status to LATAM users or stakeholders, acknowledge this gap explicitly. Do not promise Q2 improvements for LATAM.

**Key behavioral truth #4 — Resolution is binary for users:**
Users experience support as either "it worked" or "I had to talk to a person." Partial resolutions (bot answers one of three questions) are counted as escalations in user perception even if our metrics count them differently. When designing any interaction flow, optimize for complete resolution, not answer delivery.

**User need that drives the Q2 OKR:**
"When I have a support issue with Acme's product, I want it resolved immediately without having to explain myself multiple times or wait for a human agent."
